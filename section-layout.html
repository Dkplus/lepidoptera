3.1.1 Badge

Support for badges within the Navbar is built in in MaterializeCSS. But its missing support for badges near to Materialize Icons. This has been added. Together with class circle it can be used to create a nice looking notification counter.

3.2 SideNav

Slide out menu from MaterializeCSS. It has been extended with some modifiers that allow a more flexible usage of the side-nav.

The two most noteworthy things are the possibility to always have a small side-nav that expands on hover (See .side-nav--expanding and to add an logo to the sidebar.

For most use cases you can keep up using the normal markup from Materialize, but for some modifiers you need to put the side-nav class at a div and put the ul inside of this div:

<div class="side-nav">
 <ul>
   <li><a href="#!">Label</a></li>
 </ul>
</div>

Please have a look at the documentation of MaterializeCSS especially for the initialization with jQuery and the Dropdown HTML Structure.

.side-nav--expanding

On large devices it just opens a small preview of the side-nav. The full side-nav becomes visible when the user hovers the side-nav. Can be perfectly combined with .fixed to always have a small menu without loosing much space. On small and medium devices it will behave like the regular .side-nav.
